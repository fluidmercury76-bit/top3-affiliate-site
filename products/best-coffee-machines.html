How to choose the right coffee machine

Start with how you actually drink coffee at home. If you want quick, tidy and repeatable results, a pod machine makes the most sense. If you care more about hands on brewing or frothing control, you may eventually want something more involved than the options on this page.

Also consider worktop space and how often you will use the machine. A compact machine is easier to keep out and use daily, while a larger or more premium model needs to earn its place in the kitchen.

Pod coffee machine running costs

The machine price is only part of the story. Pod systems are convenient, but regular use can make the ongoing pod cost more important than the difference between one machine and another.

That does not make pod machines a bad choice. It simply means the best value buyer is not always the one who spends the least upfront, but the one who gets the easiest routine that still feels worth it over time.

Is a premium coffee machine worth it?

A premium pod machine is worth it when the improved design, finish and overall feel genuinely matter to you. For people who use a coffee machine every day, those small quality differences can add up.

If you mainly want occasional coffee with minimum spend, a value option will often be enough and may be the more sensible buy.

Frequently asked questions

What type of coffee machine is best for home use?

That depends on how involved you want to be. Pod machines are simple, bean to cup models are convenient and manual machines offer more control.

Are bean to cup coffee machines worth it?

They can be worth it if you drink coffee regularly and want fresh coffee with less effort than a manual espresso setup.

How much should I spend on a coffee machine?

A lower budget can still get you a decent machine, but spending more often brings better consistency, stronger build quality and a more polished overall experience.

Do coffee machines need a lot of cleaning?

All coffee machines need some cleaning, but some are easier than others. Machines with simpler removable parts are usually more convenient.

Can I make plant based coffees with a home machine?

Yes. Many machines work well with oat, soy or almond milk, especially if you pair them with a suitable frothing system or use drinks that do not rely on steaming.

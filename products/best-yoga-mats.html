How to choose the right yoga mat

Start with the style of practice and how your body feels on the floor. Some buyers want more cushioning for joints, while others prefer a firmer mat that feels steadier in standing work.

It also helps to think about how often you will use it. A mat that is easy to clean and pleasant to practise on will usually feel like better value over time.

How thick should a yoga mat be?

Thicker mats can feel softer and more forgiving on knees and wrists, while thinner mats often feel more stable for balance and standing poses.

There is no single perfect thickness. The best choice depends on your comfort needs and the kind of practice you do most.

Are premium yoga mats worth it?

They can be, especially for regular practitioners who care about the feel, finish and long term usability of the mat.

But many buyers are still very well served by a strong all round or value option if their needs are mainly home practice and general movement.

Frequently asked questions

How thick should a yoga mat be?

That depends on the style of practice and your comfort needs. Thicker mats can feel softer on joints, while thinner mats may feel more stable.

What is the best yoga mat for beginners?

Beginners often do well with a mat that balances grip, comfort and easy care without being too bulky or slippery.

Do expensive yoga mats last longer?

Some premium mats do offer better grip and durability, but a good mid range mat can still be excellent for regular practice.

How do I stop a yoga mat from slipping?

Choosing a mat with good grip, keeping it clean and matching the surface to your style of practice can all help reduce slipping.

Can I use a yoga mat for stretching and floor exercise too?

Yes, many yoga mats are also useful for stretching, mobility work and general home exercise.

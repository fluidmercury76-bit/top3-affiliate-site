How to choose the right patio heater for your space

Start by thinking about how open the area is. Larger exposed spaces often need stronger heat, while smaller covered areas can work well with a simpler electric or infrared model.

It also helps to be honest about convenience. A powerful heater is not always the best choice if the running style feels inconvenient every time you want to use it.

Gas, electric and infrared patio heaters compared

Gas heaters suit buyers who want stronger heat and a more traditional outdoor heating feel, especially in social spaces.

Electric models are easier to plug in and use, while infrared heaters often appeal to buyers who want more direct, efficient warmth in a modern format.

Are patio heaters worth it in the UK?

For many people, yes. They can make spring evenings, cooler summer nights and early autumn gatherings far more comfortable.

The key is choosing a heater that matches the space. A poor fit can feel disappointing, while the right fit can genuinely extend how often you use the garden or patio.

Frequently asked questions

Are patio heaters worth it in the UK?

They can be very worthwhile for extending evenings outdoors, especially in spring, autumn and cooler summer nights.

What type of patio heater is best?

The best type depends on your outdoor space, power source and whether you want portability, wall mounting or more focused heat.

Do patio heaters use a lot of energy?

Energy use varies by model and fuel type, so it is worth checking the output and running style before buying.

Can patio heaters stay outside all year?

Some are designed for outdoor use, but many last longer if covered or stored properly during poor weather.

Are electric patio heaters better than gas ones?

Electric models can be simpler and cleaner to use, while gas heaters may suit spaces where mains power is less convenient.

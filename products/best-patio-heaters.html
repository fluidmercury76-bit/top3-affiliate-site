That depends on how you sit outside. Larger social areas often suit broader heat, while smaller tables and covered spots usually work better with more direct focused warmth.

Is gas too much hassle?

For some buyers it is worth it because the stronger heat feels more outdoor ready. For others, plug in convenience matters more than maximum output.

How to choose the right patio heater for your space

Start by thinking about how open the area is. Larger exposed spaces often need stronger heat, while smaller covered areas can work well with a simpler electric or infrared model. Wind and exposure make a bigger difference than many buyers expect, which is why the same heater can feel excellent in one patio and disappointing in another.

It also helps to be honest about convenience. A powerful heater is not always the best choice if the running style feels inconvenient every time you want to use it. The easier the heater feels to turn on and use, the more likely it is that you will actually keep using the space.

Gas, electric and infrared patio heaters compared

Gas heaters suit buyers who want stronger heat and a more traditional outdoor heating feel, especially in social spaces where several people may be sitting outside together. They tend to make the biggest visual and physical impact, which is why they often feel like the main event in a patio setup.

Electric models are easier to plug in and use, which makes them more appealing for smaller patios, balconies and simpler outdoor setups. Infrared heaters often appeal to buyers who want more direct efficient warmth in a modern format, especially where the seating area is clearly defined and the goal is to warm people rather than the surrounding air.

Are patio heaters worth it in the UK?

For many people, yes. They can make spring evenings, cooler summer nights and early autumn gatherings far more comfortable. In the UK especially, outdoor spaces often feel just slightly too cool rather than completely unusable, which is exactly where a patio heater can make the difference between going inside early and staying out longer.

The key is choosing a heater that matches the space. A poor fit can feel disappointing, while the right fit can genuinely extend how often you use the garden or patio through the year.

Running practicality matters more than people expect

Running cost matters, but so does ease. A heater that is awkward to fuel, awkward to position or awkward to power can end up being used less than a slightly less powerful option that fits your routine properly. That is why convenience is such a big part of outdoor heating value.

Buyers often focus on the heat first and only later realise that access to power, storage and overall hassle have a big effect on whether the heater feels like a good purchase.

Frequently asked questions

Are patio heaters worth it in the UK?

They can be very worthwhile for extending evenings outdoors, especially in spring, autumn and cooler summer nights.

What type of patio heater is best?

The best type depends on your outdoor space, power source and whether you want portability, wall mounting or more focused heat.

Do patio heaters use a lot of energy?

Energy use varies by model and fuel type, so it is worth checking the output and running style before buying.

Can patio heaters stay outside all year?

Some are designed for outdoor use, but many last longer if covered or stored properly during poor weather.

Are electric patio heaters better than gas ones?

Electric models can be simpler and cleaner to use, while gas heaters may suit spaces where mains power is less convenient.

Which patio heater is best for a covered patio?

Electric and infrared patio heaters are often better suited to covered patios because they provide more direct warmth and are easier to use in smaller sheltered areas.

Do patio heaters work in open gardens?

They can, but open exposed gardens often reduce how effective the warmth feels. Larger gas heaters usually cope better in more open spaces than smaller electric options.

What matters most when buying a patio heater?

The most important thing is matching the heater to the space. Outdoor size, shelter, power access and the kind of warmth you want all matter more than headline output alone.

Is infrared heat better than ordinary electric heat outdoors?

It can be, especially when you want more direct focused warmth in a defined seating area rather than trying to warm the surrounding air.

Should a patio heater look decorative as well as practical?

For many buyers, yes. Outdoor heaters often become part of the space visually, so the best choice usually feels right both in heat and in how it sits within the patio or garden.

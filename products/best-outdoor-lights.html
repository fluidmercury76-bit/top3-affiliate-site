How to choose the right type of outdoor light

Start by deciding whether you want visibility, atmosphere or both. Path and wall lights are more practical, while string lights lean more towards decorative evening use.

It also helps to think about how permanent the setup should be. Some buyers want a quick flexible solution, while others want something that feels fixed and dependable.

Are solar outdoor lights worth it?

For many buyers, yes. Solar lights are easy to install, cost little to run and are especially useful in places where wiring would be awkward.

Their main trade off is consistency. They can be less reliable in darker months or heavily shaded parts of the garden.

Do outdoor lights improve security as well as style?

They can do both. Wall mounted lighting is often best for entrances and darker corners where visibility matters most.

Decorative lighting can still help a space feel more inviting, but it usually works best alongside more practical lighting if security is also a concern.

Frequently asked questions

What are the best outdoor lights for a garden?

How bright should outdoor lights be?

That depends on the job. Path lighting can be softer, while security or entrance lighting usually needs a stronger output.

Do outdoor lights need to be waterproof?

They should be properly weather suitable for outdoor use and able to cope with normal British conditions.

Can outdoor lights improve security?
